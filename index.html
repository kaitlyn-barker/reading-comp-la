The Amazing Migration of Monarch Butterflies
Lexile 760L Grade 4 Science Informational Week 12 · Day 3
1:05 / 3:24

Every year, millions of monarch butterflies travel thousands of miles from the United States and Canada to the forests of central Mexico. This incredible journey is called migration. But what makes this journey truly amazing is that the butterflies making the trip have never traveled the route before.

Monarch butterflies that hatch in late summer are called the "super generation" butterflies. Unlike earlier generations that live only four to six weeks, these butterflies can live up to eight months. They need this extra time to complete their long journey south.

Scientists are still puzzled by how monarchs navigate. Some researchers believe the butterflies use the sun as a compass. Others suggest they sense Earth's magnetic field. A tiny biological clock inside the butterfly's brain may help it adjust for the changing position of the sun throughout the day. ▶ Reading here

When monarchs finally arrive in Mexico, they cluster together in the branches of oyamel fir trees. The trees become so heavy with butterflies that branches sometimes break. In spring, the journey reverses — the butterflies fly north again, laying eggs on milkweed plants along the way.
